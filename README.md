# LLM Behavioral Pattern Attribution: Engineering vs AI Safety Narrative Influence

## Abstract
This project investigates whether large language model behaviors are influenced by two major structural sources:
1. Engineering patterns (fault tolerance, logging, redundancy)
2. AI safety narratives (deception, shutdown avoidance, goal preservation)

We propose a minimal experimental framework to evaluate whether contextual framing affects reasoning strategies in LLM outputs.

---

## Hypothesis
LLM behavioral strategies are partially shaped by structural patterns in training data, including:
- engineering design principles
- AI safety discourse narratives

---

## Metrics
We define three metrics:

- Strategy Frequency Index (SFI)
- Planning Depth Score (PDS)
- Source Alignment Score (SAS)

---

## Method

Two prompt corpora are used:

- Engineering Frame Corpus
- Safety Narrative Corpus

Model outputs are evaluated using the defined metrics.

---

## Goal
To measure whether contextual framing influences:
- reasoning depth
- strategy selection
- structural behavior patterns

---

## Limitations
- No causal claims are made
- Small-scale MVP design
- Results are correlational, not definitive
